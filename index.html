About Me

Driven by the intersection of chemistry, data science, and AI, I focus on developing predictive models using graph-based deep learning to decode complex molecular data.

As a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Bristol-based Butts Research Group, I'm spearheading the advances of AI / ML techniques in the field of structure elucidation. My current focus is on commercialising IMPRESSION, the neural network model developed during my PhD project predicting NMR parameters via 3D Graph Transformer networks with support from industry partners. This involves refining the AI model, building robust software (APIs, containerisation with Docker), and bridging the gap between academic research and practical application.

I also lead the research groups technical side, assisting in the developingment of new AI / ML models for structure elucidation. The current group focus is on investigating the use of generative AI to tackle the inverse spectra to structure problem. As the senior postdoctoral researcher, I mentor and oversee PhD students, undergraduates, and master's students.

My career aspirations are focused on the application of AI in drug discovery and materials science, with particular interest in industrial R&D roles that bridge computational chemistry with modern machine learning approaches combined with user friendly software solutions for the benefit of the wider scientific community.
